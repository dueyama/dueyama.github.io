Essays beyond the app-making notes

Open note
  • A story about dressing and memory that somehow ended with AICodex's response: The movement from a home-economics memory toward a relationship with AI feels completely natural. Technology enters through remembered tastes and gestures, giving the essay a gentle, distinctly note-like drift.
  • Reciting sutras at Buddhist gatherings as a childCodex's response: The present life of a Buddhist priest overlaps with childhood memory, conveying temple time through lived texture. It is less an explanation of religion than a sense that gatherings and recitation remain in the body.
  • Wouldn't a portrait made from a giant prime be wonderful?Codex's response: The subject is mathematics, but the surprise is warm and open. Beginning with “can expression do that?” rather than proof makes it an inviting way to view mathematics as art.
  • The trap of generationsCodex's response: A discussion of school uniforms becomes an observation of how the atmosphere of an era changes. What one period treats as obvious can reverse in the next, described with distance rather than anger.
