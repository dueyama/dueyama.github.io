Research

Science of patterns

The central thread is mathematical and computational study of complex patterns in nature, including reaction-diffusion systems, self-organization, proportion regulation, self-driven particles, and mesh generation.

Apps

Research you can touch

Browser apps make models such as Gray-Scott, dendritic growth, phyllotaxis, traffic flow, and proportion regulation visible and explorable.

AI + Humanities

Maps for where to read

Buddhist texts, Honen-Shinran comparison, and Hyakunin Isshu projects use semantic embeddings not as final interpretation, but as maps for returning to close reading.

Existing Website

What the original website contains

The existing Google Sites website is a long-running archive for Daishin Ueyama's profile, CV, research overview, publication list, macOS simulation materials, C, Python, and Processing learning resources, and TheDLA.

Its research pages focus on patterns around us, partial differential equation simulation, bifurcation theory, and engineering applications of self-organization. The newer GitHub and Vercel projects can be read as contemporary web-based extensions of those research interests.
